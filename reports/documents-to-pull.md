## FREESTONE COUNTY — COURTHOUSE VISIT REQUIRED

### CRITICAL

**1. T.A. Bounds Estate Partition Judgment**
- **Where to look**: 87th Judicial District Court minutes, Freestone County Courthouse
- **Case**: No. 8153-B, *Rufus McClelland et al. vs. J.V. Bounds Jr. et al.*
- **Date**: January 28, 1926
- **Citation**: Vol. 1, Pg. 233 or 235 (two deeds cite different pages — same judgment, likely spans multiple pages)
- **What it is**: Court-ordered partition of the T.A. Bounds estate among heirs. Divides land in the Wm. Richie Survey A-527 into numbered lots.
- **Why it matters**: Determines which heir received which lot — specifically resolves whether the 33.66-ac Beldin Tract (F15410) comes from Lot 2 or Lot 3 of the partition. This is the single most important document for closing the Beldin Tract upstream chain gap (ISSUE-44).
- **Search tips**: District court civil minutes, not deed records. Case style involves McClelland (variant: McLelland) and Bounds surnames. The partition divided at least 181.3 ac Wm. Richie A-527 into three 60.4-ac lots plus other land.

### HIGH

**2. "BOUNDS J M EST" Instrument (dated 11-11-1911)**
- **Where to look**: Freestone County deed records — citation unknown; CAD deed histories for parcels F16075, F16077, and F16074 list this as an upstream entry but provide no Vol/Pg
- **What it is**: Estate instrument for J.M. Bounds (Jesse M. Bounds, d. 8/30/1915 per GEDCOM — but 1911 date conflicts; identity uncertain)
- **Property**: B. Slaughter Survey A-565 and W. Curry Survey A-9 parcels
- **Why it matters**: Establishes how the ~492-ac Exhibit A block passed from J.M. Bounds to subsequent owners. Also helps resolve J.M. Bounds' identity (ISSUE-35) and the upstream conveyance gap to Olena (ISSUE-46).
- **Search tips**: Try FC index under "BOUNDS J M" as grantor, 1911–1915 date range. Also try "BOUNDS JESSE M" and "BOUNDS JESSE." The CAD entry says 11-11-1911 but this could be approximate. Surveys to look for: B. Slaughter A-565, W. Curry A-9, J. Strickland A-550.

### MEDIUM

**3. J.V. Bounds Jr. Will (FC Probate)**
- **Where to look**: Freestone County probate records, County Court
- **Probate No.**: 3217 (referenced in deed [319], administrator's deed by Oliver A. Bounds)
- **Date executed**: June 18, 1923 (per deed [308] recital)
- **What it is**: Last will and testament of Jesse Vastine Bounds Jr., identifying his real estate remaindermen for the 60.4-ac Wm. Richie A-527 tract
- **Why it matters**: Determines who held remainder interests in Lot 3 after Bessie Bounds' life estate (or other interest). Key to understanding the Beldin Tract chain and the Iva Inez Bounds mineral cloud (ISSUE-44).
- **Search tips**: Probate No. 3217, County Court of Freestone County. Oliver A. Bounds was administrator with will annexed. Bessie Bounds was J.V. Jr.'s wife.

## LIMESTONE COUNTY — COURTHOUSE VISIT REQUIRED

### HIGH

**4. Thomas Hare → JH Bounds — McAnulty League Source Deed**
- **Where to look**: Limestone County grantee index, search under "BOUNDS J H" or "BOUNDS JAMES H" as grantee, pre-1869
- **Grantor**: Thomas Hare (and wife)
- **Date**: Before August 3, 1869 (deed [123] references this conveyance as already completed by that date)
- **Property**: Sarah McAnulty League — acreage unknown but exceeds 200 ac (deed [123] carved the south 200 ac from this larger tract)
- **What it is**: The upstream acquisition deed for JH Bounds' primary McAnulty League holding — almost certainly the "278-acre survey" referenced as a neighbor call in deed [103] (1881)
- **Why it matters**: Importance reduced now that the Hall→Bounds deed has been pulled (478 ac). The Hare tract may be a separate acquisition or may overlap with/be part of the Hall tract. The relationship between the two will become clear once the Hall deed (DR-0000L-00219) is transcribed.
- **Search tips**: Search LC grantee index under "BOUNDS" with "HARE" as grantor, letter-volume era (Books B through H, roughly 1860s–1870s). The deed was likely recorded between 1865 and 1869. Survey name variants: "Sarah McAnulty," "S. McAnulty," "Sarah W. McNulty."

### MEDIUM

**5. LC Vol. G, Pg. 661 — Manning → JH Bounds**
- **Where to look**: Limestone County deed records
- **Citation**: Vol. G (Book G), Pg. 661
- **Grantor**: Reuben Manning
- **Grantee**: J.H. Bounds
- **Date**: July 20, 1869
- **Property**: 3 acres, McAnulty League (reduced to 2 ac by later RR right-of-way)
- **What it is**: Source deed for the 2-acre Second Tract referenced in deeds [22] and [01]
- **Why it matters**: Chain is established by reference in later deeds, but the original has never been pulled. Confirms description and verifies no competing claims or reservations (ISSUE-15).
- **Search tips**: Letter-volume era — Book G, page 661. LC CountyFusion index has NO record at this citation (likely a digitization gap). Try searching under "MANNING REUBEN" as grantor or "BOUNDS" as grantee in Book G.

**6. Lou Martin Probate — Limestone County**
- **Where to look**: Limestone County probate records (primary); also check Freestone County
- **Decedent name variants**: Louise Martin, Lou Martin, Mrs. J.M. Bounds Martin, L.C. Martin, "Bounds-Martin J M Mrs" (LC index form)
- **Date range**: ~1953–1960
- **Background**: Family oral history (Kay Black, 2026-04-05) confirms Lou died mid-to-late 1950s while living with Olena and JP Black in Limestone County. LC deed records confirm she was active under "BOUNDS-MARTIN J M MRS" through at least 1948. FC deed records confirm she held J. Strickland Survey minerals (Exhibit A First Tract) as late as 1950 and gave POA to Mattie Bounds in August 1956.
- **What it is**: Probate file identifying what land Lou held at death and how it passed (presumably to Olena as sole heir — no children from either marriage)
- **Why it matters**: Lou's estate is the most likely conduit for the ~492-ac Exhibit A block reaching Olena. All nine Exhibit A tracts trace to J.M. Bounds (= Jesse M. Bounds, Lou's first husband). If Lou inherited Jesse's land on his death in 1915, held it for 40 years, and it passed to Olena on Lou's death in the mid-1950s, that closes the ISSUE-46 gap entirely (ISSUE-16).
- **Search tips**: Search probate index under surname "MARTIN" (Lou's surname at death). Also search LC deed index under "BOUNDS-MARTIN" and "MARTIN LOU" as grantor, 1950–1960. Lou had no children — Olena (her niece) is the most likely sole heir.
