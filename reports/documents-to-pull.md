## FREESTONE COUNTY

### CRITICAL

**1. T.A. Bounds Estate Partition Judgment**
- **Where to look**: 87th Judicial District Court minutes, Freestone County Courthouse
- **Case**: No. 8153-B, *Rufus McClelland et al. vs. J.V. Bounds Jr. et al.*
- **Date**: January 28, 1926
- **Citation**: Vol. 1, Pg. 233 or 235 (two deeds cite different pages — same judgment, likely spans multiple pages)
- **What it is**: Court-ordered partition of the T.A. Bounds estate among heirs. Divides land in the Wm. Richie Survey A-527 into numbered lots.
- **Why it matters**: Determines which heir received which lot — specifically resolves whether the 33.66-ac Beldin Tract (F15410) comes from Lot 2 or Lot 3 of the partition. This is the single most important document for closing the Beldin Tract upstream chain gap (ISSUE-44).
- **Search tips**: District court civil minutes, not deed records. Case style involves McClelland (variant: McLelland) and Bounds surnames. The partition divided at least 181.3 ac Wm. Richie A-527 into three 60.4-ac lots plus other land.

## NOTES

- **FC** = Freestone County (courthouse in Fairfield, TX)
- **LC** = Limestone County (courthouse in Groesbeck, TX)
- **DR** = Deed Records; **OR** = Official Records
- **Survey abbreviations**: A-### = Abstract number
- **Online portals**: LC uses CountyFusion; FC uses PublicSearch — both have known indexing gaps and errors documented throughout the project
- Citations in the format "Vol. X, Pg. Y" or "Book X, Pg. Y" refer to physical deed record volumes
- "Letter volumes" (Book D, Book F, Book G, Book S, etc.) are the oldest LC records from the 1860s-1890s
