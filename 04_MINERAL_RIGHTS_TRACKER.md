## How to Read This Document

Each tract is listed with its current surface owner, current mineral owner(s), known severances, and confidence level. Confidence ratings:
- **HIGH**: Documented by recorded instrument in the file set
- **MEDIUM**: Inferred from context; needs confirmation
- **LOW**: Unknown or conflicting

Texas legal concepts that affect interpretation:
- **Duhig Rule**: If a grantor reserves minerals but doesn't own the full mineral estate, the deed may be construed to transfer more surface than intended
- **After-acquired title**: If a grantor later acquires the interest they didn't have at time of conveyance, it passes to grantee
- **Executive rights**: The right to execute oil and gas leases, which can be separated from the mineral interest itself
- **Royalty vs. mineral interest**: A royalty is a fraction of production; a mineral interest includes the ability to enter, develop, and receive royalties
