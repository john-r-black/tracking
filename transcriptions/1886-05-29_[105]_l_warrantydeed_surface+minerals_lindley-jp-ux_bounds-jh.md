The State of Texas County of Limestone} Know all men by these presents that we J. P. Lindley and wife Mary M. Lindley of said State and county for and in consideration of the sum of five hundred and fifty dollars to us in hand
paid by J. H. Bounds of said State and County have granted bargained and sold and by these presents do grant sell and convey unto the said J. H. Bounds the following described parcel of land: a part of a 99 acre tract - a part of the Samuel Holloway survey situated in Limestone County Texas deeded to Mary M. Lindley by deed of gift- from said J. H. Bounds and M. E. Bounds on the 13th day of March 1878, this part being the S.E.&. North portion of said 99 acre tract and containing 69 acres of land more or less. Said 99 acre tract being more minutely described in said J. H. Bounds and M. E. Bounds deed recorded in Books "M" deed records of Limestone county pages 390, 391 & 392. To have and to hold all and singular the above described property with the rights thereunto pertaining unto the said J. H. Bounds his heirs and assigns forever- and we do by these presents bind ourselves our heirs executors and assigns to forever warrant and defend the title to the said land unto the said J. H. Bounds his heirs and assigns against the lawful claims of all persons claiming or to claim the same. In witness whereof we have hereunto set our hands this 29 day of May A.D. 1886
J. P. Lindley
M. M. Lindley

The State of Texas }
County of Freestone } Before me W. Allegra a Justice of the peace and ex officio Notary Public in and for Freestone county on this day personally appeared J. P. Lindley Known to me to be the person whose name is subscribed to the foregoing instrument of writing and acknowledged to me that he executed the same for the purposes and consideration therein expressed, Also before me on this day personally appeared Mary M. Lindley Known to me to be the wife of J. P. Lindley and to be the person whose name is subscribed to the foregoing instrument of writing and being examined by me privily and apart from her said husband and having the same by me fully explained to her. she the said Mary M. Lindley acknowledged such instrument to be her act and deed and declared that she had willingly signed the same for the purposes and consideration therein expressed and that she did not wish to retract it. Given under my hand and seal of office at Wortham Texas this 29th day of May 1886
(L.S.)
W. Allegra J.P. & Ex officio
Notary Public. Freestone Co. Tex.

I certify that the foregoing is a true & correct copy of the original deed & certificate of acknowledgement filed for record in my office Dec. 12 1887 at 9 am. and recorded Dec. 17th 1887 at 5 P.M.
W. F. Brown Co. Clk.
By H. Williams Dept.
