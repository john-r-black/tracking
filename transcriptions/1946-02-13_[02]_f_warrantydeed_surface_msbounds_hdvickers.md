WARRANTY DEED

M. S. BOUNDS ET UX TO HUGH DAVID VICKERS
THE STATE OF TEXAS
COUNTY OF FREESTONE
KNOW ALL MEN BY THESE PRESENTS:
THAT We, M. S. Bounds, and wife, Mattie Bounds, of the County of Freestone State of Texas, for and in consideration of the sum of SIX HUNDRED ($600.00) DOLLARS to us paid, and secured to be paid, by Hugh David Vickers, as follows: TWO HUNDRED ($200.00) DOLLARS cash in hand paid by the grantee to the grantors, the receipt of which is hereby acknowledged and confessed; and the further consideration of the execution by the grantee of Three (3) vendor's lien notes of even date herewith, payable to M. S. Bounds, or order, at Wortham, Texas, with interest thereon from date until maturity at the rate of Eight (8%) per annum, the interest payable annually as it accrues, Note No. 1 in the sum of $100.00, due on or before December 1, 1946, Note No. 2, in the sum of $150.00, due on or before December 1, 1947 Note No. 3, in the sum of $150.00, due on or before December 1, 1948, All past due interest shall bear interest from maturity hereof, at the rate of ten per cent per annum, and containing the accelerating clause, and providing for attorney fees for collection, or if collected by suit, or through the Probate or Bankruptcy proceedings of ten per cent on the principal then due and the interest then due, etc. have Granted, Sold and Conveyed, and by these presents do Grant, Sell and Convey, unto the said Hugh David Vickers, of the County of Rusk State of Texas, all that certain lot, tract or parcel of land situated in Freestone County, Texas, being a part of the J. F. McGuffin Survey, Patent No. 456, Abstract No. 406, and more particularly described as follows, A part of the 112.60 acres conveyed by Mrs. Jane Jordan to Chas. and Harrison Morgan.
 
BEGINNING at a stake the N.W. corner of Melia Smith's 25.00 acre tract (out of said 112.60 acres);

THENCE N35°00'00"E 292.00 vrs. to the S. W. corner of the 43.60 acres set apart to J. M. Jordan et als.

THENCE S55°00'00"E 400.00 vrs. with the S. line of J. M. Jordan et als tract to a stake in road.

THENCE S57°00'00"E 321.00 vrs. a stake Melia Smith's N.E. corner;

THENCE N55°00'00"W 520.00 vrs. with Melia Smith's North line to place of beginning. Also Two (2) small tracts lying north of the above described tract being woodland and being the same land set apart to Chas. Morgan in Cause No. 3656 in the District court of Freestone County, styled Chas. Morgan vs. J. M. Jordan et als, as surveyed for Chas. Morgan by T. Bennett out of said 112.60 acre tract, a better description of which cannot be given, but same is the land heretofore claimed by Chas. Morgan and used and occupied by him and the field notes as made by T. Bennett, surveyor, are here referred to and made a part hereof for description of same; said Woodland tract containing about Eleven (11.00) acres, and all said land to contain 43.00 acres, more or less, and being the same land conveyed to grantors herein by warranty deed dated May 15, 1917, recorded in Vol. 50 page 387 Deed Records of Freestone County, Texas, to which deed and the record thereof reference is hereby made for all purposes.

It is hereby expressly agreed and understood, that out of the grant hereby made, there is excepted and reserved to the grantors herein a one-half of the one-eighth or a one-sixteenth royalty interest in all mines of, and all oil, gas and all minerals, on and under the said land and premises herein described and conveyed.

TO HAVE AND TO HOLD the above described premises, together with all and singular the rights and appurtenances thereto in anywise belonging unto the said Hugh David Vickers, his heirs and assigns forever and we do hereby bind ourselves, our heirs, executors and administrators, to Warrant and Forever Defend, all and singular the said premises unto the said Hugh David Vickers, his heirs and assigns, against every person whomsoever lawfully claiming, or to claim the same, or any part thereof.

But it is expressly agreed and stipulated that the Vendor's Lien is retained against the above described property, premises and improvements, until the above described notes, and all interest thereon are fully paid according to their face and tenor effect and reading when this deed shall become absolute.
 
WITNESS our hand at Wortham, Texas, this 13 day of February A. D. 1946. 
M. S. Bounds
($1.10 Federal Revenue attached and cancelled.) Mattie Bounds 

THE STATE OF TEXAS
COUNTY OF FREESTONE
BEFORE ME, the undersigned, a Notary Public in and for said County and state, on this day personally appeared M. S. Bounds and Mattie Bounds, his wife, both known to me to be the persons whose names are subscribed to the foregoing instrument, and acknowledged to me that they each executed the same for the purposes and consideration therein expressed, and the said Mattie Bounds, wife of the said M. S. Bounds having been examined by me privily and apart from her husband, and having the same fully explained to her, she, the said Mattie Bounds acknowledged such instrument to be her act and deed, and she declared that she had willingly signed the same for the purposes and consideration therein expressed, and that she did not wish to retract it.

GIVEN UNDER MY HAND AND SEAL OF OFFICE this the 13 day of February, A. D. 1946.
Walter T. Thomason, Notary Public in and for
(SEAL) Freestone County, Texas.
