that I am above the age of eighteen years. That GUSSIE WEAVER was
my father. GUSSIE WEAVER was married only one time to JEWELL P.
WEAVER, my mother. They were married March 4, 1914 at Richland,
Texas. Of this marriage two children were born: myself, JOSIE
EVELYN WEAVER, born March 8, 1915 and my brother, TED A. WEAVER,
born December 19, 1933.

GUSSIE WEAVER died intestate August 7, 1975 and no
probate proceedings were held in connection with his estate as he
owed no outstanding debts and the estate was too small to require
payment of estate or inheritance taxes, being only of a value of
approximately $25,000.00, consisting of cash and land.

My father used this property continuously from that date up
until his death in 1975. GUSSIE WEAVER used this property for
farming and ranching purposes, always kept it under fence and
always paid all taxes timely. After his death my mother, JEWELL
P. WEAVER, used this property for farming and ranching purposes
until she sold it to my brother, TED A. WEAVER, December 23, 1980,
said deed recorded January 17, 1985, Volume 693, Page 131, Deed
Records, Freestone County, Texas. Since that time TED A. WEAVER
has continued to use this property for farming and ranching
purposes, he has kept this property under fence and paid all taxes
timely.

From the year 1930 up until the present time the above
described property has been used, occupied and claimed by the
Weaver family from GUSSIE WEAVER up until and including the
present owner, TED A. WEAVER. They have promptly paid all taxes
on said property as they become due. Their claim was notorious in
the community and they claimed adversely to the whole world, and I
never heard anyone dispute their claim of ownership and feel sure
that I would have should it have ever been done.

The information contained herein concerning the estate
of GUSSIE WEAVER is true and correct to the best of my belief and
knowledge.
