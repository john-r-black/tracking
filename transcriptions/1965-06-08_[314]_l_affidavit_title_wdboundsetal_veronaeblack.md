THE STATE OF TEXAS

COUNTY OF LIMESTONE

BEFORE ME, the undersigned authority, on this day personally appeared W. D. Bounds and Jeff Bounds, affiants, and on their oaths say that they are each over twenty-one years of age and are not incapacitated in any way; and that the following facts are true and correct: That affiants are each well and personally acquainted with a 56.8 acre tract of land out of the Lipscomb Norvell Survey, Abstract No. 20, Limestone County, Texas, described as follows:

BEING 56.8 acres of land, more or less, out of the Lipscomb Norvell Survey, Abstract No. 20: BEGINING at a stake at T. W. Wades S. W. corner in the South line of a 400 acre survey bought by John Caruthers from said Lipscomb Norvell; THENCE South 430 vrs. to a stake in the North line of Jones 150 acre survey; THENCE East with said line 745-8/10 vrs. to Jones N. E. corner; THENCE North 430 vrs. to stake in South line of John Caruthers 400 acre survey; THENCE West 745-8/10 vrs. to the place of beginning, CONTAINING 56.8 acres of land, more or less.

That affiants state that said property was acquired by W. E. Black and wife, Ida A. Black more than 40 years ago and that in the year 1943, W. E. Black and his children conveyed all their interest in said property to J. T. Black and that after the death of J. T. Black in 1959, his children conveyed said property to Verona E. Black, the widow of J. T. Black; that the said Verona E. Black and those under shom (sic) she claims have claimed said property under their deeds thereto, properly recorded in the County Clerk's office, Limestone County, Texas; that the various parties have claimed said property as their own under such deeds and adversely to everyone and during the past 40 years, the owners of said property have been in possession of the property and improvements thereon either in person or through tenants, and their use of same has been uninterrupted, and with their claim, has been notorious in the community. That the outside boundaries of said property have been well established and defined by fences capable of turning cattle and that for the past 40 years the possession by the said parties and the persons under whom they claim such property has been
