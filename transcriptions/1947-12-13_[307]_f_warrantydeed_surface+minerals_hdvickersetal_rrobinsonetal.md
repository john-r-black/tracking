HUGH DAVID VICKERS ET UX.        TO        RAY ROBINSON ET UX

THE STATE OF TEXAS    }
COUNTY OF TITUS       }    KNOW ALL MEN BY THESE PRESENTS:

THAT WE, Hugh David Vickers, and wife, Olga Brand Vickers of the County of Titus
State of Texas for and in consideration of the sum of TEN DOLLARS ($10.00) DOLLARS to us paid
and secured to be paid
by Ray Robinson and Georgie Lee Robinson as follows; Ten Dollars ($10.00) cash, the receipt
of which is hereby acknowledged, and the assumption of three vendor lien notes of date February
13, 1946, and due and payable as follows:
    Note No. I, in the sum of $100.00 (one hundred dollars) payable on the first day of
December, 1946, Note No. 2, in the sum of $150.00 (one hundred and fifty dollars) due and payable
on or before December 1st, 1947 Note No. 3, in the sum of $150.00 (one hundred and fifty dollars,
due on or before December 1st, 1948,
    Each of the above notes provide for 8% interest per annum, interest payable annually
as is shown by the deed records of Freestone County, Volumn 193, Page 96, to which reference is
here made for all legal purposes.
have Granted, Sold and Conveyed, and by these presents do Grant, Sell and Convey, unto the said
Ray Robinson and Georgie Lee Robinson of the County of Limestone State of Texas all that certain
lot, tract or parcel of land situated in Freestone County, Texas, being a part of the J. F. McGuf-
fin Survey, Patent No. 456, Abstract No. 406, and more particularly described as follows, A part
of the 112.6 acres conveyed by Mrs. Jane Jordan to Chas. and Harrison Morgan.
    BEGINNING at a stake the N.W. corner of Melia Smith's 25 acre tract (Out of said 112.6)
THENCE N. 35 E. 292 vrs. to the S. W. corner of the 43.6 acres set apart to J. M. Jordan et als.
THENCE S. 55 E. 400 vrs. with the S. line of J. M. Jordan et als tract to a stake in road.
THENCE S. 57 E. 321 vrs. a stake Melia Smith's N. E. corner; THENCE N. 55 W. 520 vrs. with Melia
Smith's North line to place of beginning. Also two (2) small tracts lying north of the above
described tract being woodland and being the same land set apart to Chas. Morgan in Cause No.
3656 in the District Court of Freestone County, styled Chas. Morgan vs. J. M. Jordan et als, as
surveyed for Chas. Morgan by T. Bennett out of said 112.6 acre tract, a better description of
which cannot be given, but same is the land heretofore claimed by Chas. Morgan and used an (sic) occup-
ied by him and the field notes as made by T. Bennett surveyor are here referred to and made a
part hereof for description of same; said woodland tract containing about Eleven (11) acres,

and all said land to contain 43 acre, more or less, and being the same land conveyed to grantors
herein by warranty deed dated May 15, 1917, recorded in Vol. 50, page 387, Deed Records of
Freestone County, Texas, to which deed and the record thereof reference is hereby made for
all purposes.
    It is hereby expressly agreed and understood, that out of the grant hereby made, there
is excepted and reserved to the grantors herein a one-half of the one-eighth or a one-sixteenth
royalty interest in all mines of, and all oil, gas and all minerals, on and under the said land
and premises herein described and conveyed.
    TO HAVE AND TO HOLD the above described premises, together with all and singular the
rights and appurtenances thereto in anywise belonging unto the said Ray Robinson and Georgie
Lee Robinson heirs and assigns forever and we do hereby bind ourselves, our heirs, executors and
administrators, to Warrant and Forever Defend, all and singular the said premises unto the
said Ray Robinson, his heirs and assigns, against every person whomsoever lawfully claiming, or
to claim the same, or any part thereof.
    But it is expressly agreed and stipulated that the Vendor's Lien is retained against
the above described property, premises and improvements, until the above described notes, and
all interest thereon are fully paid according to their face and tenor, effect and reading, when
this deed shall become absolute.
    WITNESS our hand_ at Mt. Pleasant, Texas this 13th day of December, A. D. 1947.
                                            Hugh David Vickers
                                            Olga Brand Vickers

(55¢ Federal Revenue attached and cancelled.)
THE STATE OF TEXAS    }
COUNTY OF TITUS       }

BEFORE ME, the undersigned, a Notary Public in and for said County and State, on this
day personally appeared Hugh David Vickers and Olga Brand Vickers, his wife, both known to me
to be the persons whose names are subscribed to the foregoing instrument, and acknowledged to
me that they each executed the same for the purposes and consideration therein expressed, and
the said Olga Bond Vickers wife of the said Hugh David Vickers having been examined by me pri-
vily and apart from her husband, and having the same fully explained to her, she the said Olga
Brand Vickers acknowledged such instrument to be her act and deed, and she declared that she
had willingly signed the same for the purposes and consideration therein expressed, and that
she did not wish to retract it.
    GIVEN UNDER MY HAND AND SEAL OF OFFICE this the 13 day of December A. D. 1947.
(SEAL)                                      John A. Cook, Notary Public in and for
                                            Titus County, Texas.
