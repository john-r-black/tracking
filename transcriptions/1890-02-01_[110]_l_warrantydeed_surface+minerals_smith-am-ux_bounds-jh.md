[A. M. Smith & wife

J. H. Bounds
May 24 1890
 "   " 1890]

The State of Texas County of Limestone} Know all men by these presents that we A. M. Smith and wife Mary Smith formerly Mary Bennett and daughter of W. J. Bennett Deed, of the County of Navarro State of Tex for and in consideration of the sum of ninety one + 57/100 dollars to us in hand paid by J. H. Bounds the receipt of which is hereby acknowledged have granted sold and conveyed and by these presents do grant sell and convey unto the said J. H. Bounds of the county of Limestone State of Texas all that certain nine and one eighth acre land off the south end of 28 1/2 acres land deeded to W. J. Bennett by J. O. Longbotham out * of the Sarah McAnulty headright survey more particularly described as follows: Cornering with the Manning land on the east and J. H. Bounds * on the south and J. O. Longbotham on the west situated in Limestone County Tex being our entire interest in said tract, the above 9 1/4 acres also covers our interest in an other tract of 44 1/2 acres out of the said McAnulty Survey it being the W. J. Bennett old homestead which makes our 1/8 interest in both tracts to have and to hold the above described premises together with all and singular the rights and appurtenances thereto in anywise belonging unto the said J. H. Bounds his heirs and assigns forever and we do hereby bind our heirs executors and administrators to warrant and forever defend all and singular the said premises unto the said J. H. Bounds his heirs and assigns against every person whomsoever lawfully claiming or to claim the same or any part thereof. Witness our hands at Wortham this 1 day of Feb A.D. 1890
A. M. Smith
Mary Smith

The State of Texas ( Before me J. E. Longbotham a notary Public in and for Freestone County Texas on this day personally appeared A. M. Smith Known to me to be the person whose name is subscribed to the foregoing instrument and acknowledged to me that he executed the same for the purposes and consideration therein expressed. Given under my hand and seal of office this 1st day of Feb. A.D. 1890 -
(L.S.)
J. E. Longbotham N. P.
Limestone Co. Tex.

The State of Texas ( Before me J. E. Longbotham a notary Public in and for Freestone County Texas on this day personally appeared Mary Smith wife of A. M. Smith Known to me to be the person whose name is subscribed to the foregoing instrument and having been examined by me privily and apart from her husband and having the same fully explained to her she the said Mary Smith acknowledged such instrument to be her act and deed and declared that she had willingly signed the same for the purposes and consideration therein expressed and that she did not wish to retract it. Given under my hand and seal of office this 1st day of Feb. A.D. 1890 -
(L.S.)
J. E. Longbotham
N. P. Freestone Co. Tex.

I certify that the foregoing is a true & correct copy of the original deed & certificate of Authentication filed for record in my office May 24th 1890 at 5 P.M. & recorded May 26th 1890 at 9 oclock a m
W. F. Brown co clerk
By H. Williams Dept.
