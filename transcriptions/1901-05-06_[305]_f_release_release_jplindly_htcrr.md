[MARGIN: J. P. Lindley / To Release / H. & T.C.R.R. embankment. / May 8. 1901.]

The State of Texas }
County of Freestone } Know all men by these presents, that the Houston and Texas Central Railroad Company has its line of road, roadbed and embankment constructed upon and across the following described tract of land owned by J. P. Lindly in Limestone County, Texas, to-wit; a tract of 103 acres out of the Sarah McAnulty League which said tract was conveyed to J. M. Lindly by H. Clay Dunbar on May 20th 1870 by deed recorded in Book "H". pages 338 and 339 Limestone County Records of Deeds and was conveyed to the said J. P. Lindly by J. M. Lindly and wife A. Lindly by deed dated January 14th 1884 and recorded in Vol. W pages 498 and 499 Limestone County Records of Deeds, to which said deeds reference is here made for more particular description of the property herein referred to, and whereas, it is alleged and claimed by me that the openings for the passage of water through said roadbed and embankment is not sufficient to carry off the water thereby causing it to overflow and inundate my lands and damage the crops grown thereon, and whereas I have agreed with the said Houston and Texas Central Railroad Company in consideration of the payment of the sum of one hundred and 0%00 ($100.00) dollars to release said Railroad Company from any and all claims for damages of whatsoever character to my lands, premises and crops which may have accrued or which may accrue at any time hereafter. Now therefore, I J. P. Lindly, in consideration of the sum of one hundred and 0%00 ($100.00) dollars to me in hand paid by the Houston and Texas Central Railroad Company the receipt of which is hereby acknowledged, have this day released, acquitted and discharged, and by these presents do release, acquit, and discharge the said Houston and Texas Central Railroad Company, its successors and assigns from any and all claims for damages to said land and crops which have heretofore accrued or which may hereafter accrue by reason of the overflow of said lands or from any other

character or kind of injuries alleged to have been occasioned by the construction and maintenance of said railroad, roadbed and embankment and I do by these presents bind myself, my heirs, executors, administrators and assigns to forever release, acquit and discharge the said Houston and Texas Central Railroad Company, its successors and assigns from any and all damages of whatsoever character which may hereafter accrue to the said land and crops grown thereon, the intention of this instrument being to release the said Railroad Company from any and all damages which I claim to have suffered in the past and to acknowledge a full and complete satisfaction and acquittance for any and all damage to any of my lands and crops grown thereon, which may occur in the future by reason of any overflow, inundation or other cause so that neither myself, my heirs, successors or assigns shall at any time hereafter make any claim, demand or charge against the Houston and Texas Central Railroad Company, its successors or assigns for damages to my property.

Witness my hand this the 6th day of May A. D. 1901.

J. P. Lindly-

The State of Texas }
County of Freestone } Before me R. G. Leetch a notary Public in and for the County of Freestone, State of Texas on this day personally appeared J. P. Lindly, known to me to be the person whose name is subscribed to the foregoing instrument of writing and acknowledged to me that he executed the same for the purposes and consideration therein expressed.

Given under my hand and seal of office this the 6 day of May A. D. 1901.

(L.S.)                    R. G. Leetch, a notary Public
                    Freestone County, Texas

Filed for record May 8th 1901 at 8 Oclock a. m. and duly recorded May 10th 1901 at 11 Oclock a m.

J. A. Wright Co. Clk.
By S. H. Wright Dy.
